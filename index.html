Introduction

In 2020, Tuguegarao City faced a double typhoon, namely, Goni (‘aka’ Rolly) followed by Vamco (‘aka’ Ulysses) unleashing unprecedented flooding, exposing the Philippines' vulnerability to such disasters. Goni's onslaught, in October, led to severe landslides and overwhelming floods causing the blockage of drainage systems. Vamco, in November, highlighted the inadequacy of the drainage system, unable to handle the severe rain. “The consecutive storms present double trouble for the Philippines’ overcrowded evacuation centers — usually tent setups in gyms and schools — as the coronavirus continues to spread. The country surpassed 402,000 cases this week, the second-highest number in Southeast Asia.” (Cabato, 2020). The severe flooding from Typhoons Vamco and Goni in Tuguegarao City emphasised region's susceptibility to climate hazards, emphasising the urgent need for integrated disaster management, climate resilience, and improved preparedness using innovative solutions. Sustainable Urban Drainage Systems (SUDS), with their natural drainage tactics, offer an alternative to conventional systems that often lead to high flood risks. “SUDS apply natural drainage tactics, unlike most conventional systems, which commonly result in high storm water flows with successive flood risk. To increase catchment response time, they use seminatural such as infiltration trenches, swales, and ponds to delay the flows and minimise flood risks” (Jha, Bloch, & Lamond, 2012). Apart from flood prevention, SUDS also offer other benefits. This helps to increase water quality, better urban environments, and wildlife habitat preservation as well. These techniques are popularly referred to as SUDS in the UK but also using different labels like BMPS or water-sensitive urban design in respective countries elsewhere. “SUDS devices are most effective in combination, in the form of a ‘management train’” (Jha, Bloch, & Lamond, 2012) that helps to manage the stormwater in small sub- catchments at low cost. They utilize small, decentralised systems and avoid the large, centralised drainage schemes for exceptional circumstances only. Nevertheless, SUDS design should factor in the probability of groundwater contamination if surface runoff were to pollute water sources used for human consumption. Hence, SUDS provide flexibility, for instance, there are permeable pavement systems that can either allow or prohibit infiltration based on contamination worries.
